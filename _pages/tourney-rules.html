Note You are responsible for staying up to date with the ruleset and announcements at all times.

A. General Rules

  1. The use of BAC Anticheat will be mandatory during the tournament.
  2. Your entire team must be in the OCC Events Discord server for the duration of the match.
  3. Players must show up to Discord at least 15 minutes before the start of the first match.
  4. Any players having problems of any kind, can ask for help in #help-desk. A Staff member will resolve it shortly.
  5. Substitutes are not allowed to communicate with playing teammates during the match.
  6. Substitutes must be muted if they are on their team’s channel.
  7. Players must be ready to start immediately at the assigned time. Players who are not in their voice channel will be considered absent from the event.
  8. Report suspicious players or rule breakers using /report.
  9. Receiving tactical information from any service is not allowed.
  10. Substitutes are not permitted to enter the match if they are or were watching the streaming.
  11. Client versions 1.7.X to 1.12.X are allowed, any later versions (1.13or later) are not.
  12. Abusing any game-breaking bugs, exploits or glitches is considered cheating. Exploiting any behavior that a gamemode does not intend may be considered illegal. Please consult a Head of Events ahead of time on whether a given strategy is allowed.
  13. The Heads of Events reserve the right to remove any player or team from an event and reserves the right to Tournament Ban any player or team at any time if they break the spirit of this rules or threaten the welfare of the tournament.
  14. Do not use someone else’s account to register or play and do not give your account out for others to use in relation to the event.
  15. Each individual may only have one account registered and may only be registered for one team per event.
  16. Teams must have at least 6 players ready to show up at the assigned times.
  17. Matches will not be run unless all teams have the minimum required amount of players online and available to actively play the match (exceptions may be made with the veto decider map).
  18. Players are allowed to stream their first-person perspective, both publicly (i.e Twitch) or in their Voice Channel at their own risk.
  19. Butterfly clicking is strongly discouraged and may result in a ban. Proceed at your own risk.
  20. Players who are found to be cheating will be banned from all OCC services.

B. Screenshare Rules

  1. Players may be asked to screenshare if they are suspected of using an illegal advantage.
  2. Players must remove all Minecraft cheats (Damage indicator included) and Autoclickers at least one hour before the tournament.
  3. Players who have the possibility to change the "Debounce Time" of their mouse must set it to no less than 10ms to reduce the possibility of double clicks.
  4. Players must wait at least 1 minute after each game's end before leaving the server.
  5. Players must not delete or change anything (programs, files, mouse settings, etc.) during or after the game
  6. Refusing to screenshare will result in a Tournament Ban.
  7. As soon as a player is brought into the Screenshare room, they must activate Discord’s screenshare feature immediately and without further inquiry. The entire screen must be shared.
  8. After that, the Staff Team will continue the screen sharing with Anydesk. Players must activate the mouse and keyboard control.
  9. The Staff Team has several screen sharing tools at their disposal. Not downloading them will be considered denying to screenshare.
  10. Any player found editing/deleting anything, having cheats/autoclickers or using any program that blocks our screen sharing tools will receive a Tournament Ban.
  11. Intentionally delaying the screenshare may result in a Punishment.
  12. Any screenshare blockers (firewalls, programs designed to mislead and block screenshare tools, etc.) will be considered an attempt to cloud the screenshare, and may be faced with a Tournament Ban.

C. Infractions

The infracions system works as follows:

  1. Warnings: For any minor offence, the player will receive a warning. This is mainly due to toxicity, spamming or any minor block glitching that does not change the outcome of the match (e.g, block glitching to avoid death in mid). A fourth warning will result in a punishment for that player.
  2. Punishments: The player will miss the next map for the first punishment and the rest of the series for the second one. The Staff Team will be giving punishments for things like extreme toxicity (harassment, being extremely toxic, etc), stream sniping, block glitching and changing the outcome of a match (e.g. getting out of the wool while block glitching, getting into the wool block glitching, getting proximity that changes the final result while block glitching, etc.) or by receiving 3 warnings. A third punishment will result in a Tournament Ban.
  3. Tournament Ban: This is the worst type of punishment a player may get. A team can receive up to three Tournament Bans before being disqualified from the tournament. This is reserved for extreme cases such as hacking or receiving up to 3 punishments. For the first two Tournament Bans, the player will be disqualified from the tournament and the team will be given a loss for every map that player has played during that series. For the third one, the team will be disqualified from the tournament. All players being issued a Tournament Ban will be blacklisted from future tournaments.

D. Blacklist

  • Blacklisted players will not be allowed to register in future tournaments. You can try to appeal your punishment by contacting any Head of Events.

E. Void Matches

A match may be declared void if:

  1. Event servers and/or services have technical difficulties.
  2. Unforeseen events cause severe impacts to the match.
  3. A team or player is disqualified.
  4. The match ended early for any reason.
  5. The Heads of Events decide for any reason.

A void match may be:

  1. Replayed.
  2. Declared a win by default by the judgment of the Heads of Events.

F. Spectator Passes

  1. Players who have purchased any spectator pass are forbidden from affecting the outcome of the match in any way while they are spectating.
  2. In the unfortunate event that this occurs, that player may have its pass removed without a refund and the match may be voided.
  3. If the player is enlisted in the tournament, its team may be punished and that player will receive a Tournament Ban, including a blacklistfrom future events.
